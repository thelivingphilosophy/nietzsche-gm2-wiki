## Key Structural Notes

**Cruelty spiritualizes, it doesn't disappear.** The arrow between the two threads at §5–7 is the essay's hidden hinge. The same drive — pleasure in making-suffer — powers the economic thread (outward: pain as payment) and then migrates to power the psychological thread (inward: self-torture as bad conscience). By §22 it has become the energy of maximum guilt.

**The method break (§12–13) is a pause, not a mechanism.** Nietzsche steps back to explain the interpretive principle — origin never determines purpose, will to power constantly reuses old forms — before delivering the negative result at §14–15. It is scaffolding, not a new thread.

**The convergence at §19 requires both threads.** Bad conscience alone is a pre-moral wound with no vocabulary. Creditor-debtor logic alone is external discipline with no inner life. Neither produces moral guilt. Christianity fuses them — and then the trap at §21 makes the fusion permanent.

**The Greek alternative (§23) is not a digression.** It proves the Christian outcome was contingent. Same ingredients, opposite result. Without this proof, the redirect at §24 has no logical ground.
