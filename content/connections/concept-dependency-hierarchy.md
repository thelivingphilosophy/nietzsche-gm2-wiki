## Three Things the Diagram Reveals

### 1. Punishment appears at two levels

This is the essay's most counterintuitive move and the key to §12–13 (form vs. meaning).

As **mnemotechnics** (§3), punishment is *upstream* of creditor-debtor — it creates the memory of the will that makes contracts and exchange possible in the first place. As **community enforcement** (§9), it is *downstream* of creditor-debtor — the practice by which the community-as-creditor extracts payment from lawbreakers. Same word, same physical act, different mechanisms, different purposes. Origin never determines purpose. This is precisely why Nietzsche can say punishment has twelve legitimate meanings and none of them is "producing guilt" — the form is so old it has been recruited for uses its originators never imagined.

### 2. Bad conscience and Schuld are independent

They come from completely different places:

- **Schuld** flows from the economic thread: creditor-debtor → moral vocabulary. It gives you the *concepts* of guilt, obligation, debt.
- **Bad conscience** flows from the psychological thread: blond beast conquest → blocked instincts → self-directed cruelty. It gives you the *inner wound* capable of suffering.

Neither produces moral guilt alone. Bad conscience without Schuld is aimless self-torment with no moral framework — suffering with no concept of *owing*. Schuld without bad conscience is external social discipline with no interiority — debt without the *feeling* of guilt. §14–15 proves this by showing that punishment (the economic thread's enforcement arm) cannot produce the feeling. Something else is needed.

### 3. The sovereign individual is not just downstream of bad conscience

It requires bad conscience to have been *integrated*, not merely undergone. Most humans complete Stage 2 and remain there — the memory of the will is external and the inner wound never closes. The sovereign individual is the rare case where the memory of the will has become genuinely internal (owned rather than imposed) and the self-cruelty of bad conscience has been transformed into self-mastery rather than self-laceration. See [[connections/from-self-laceration-to-self-formation]] for the precise mechanism.

## What the Hierarchy Does Not Show

**Cruelty's double role.** Cruelty drives both threads — outward as the pleasure in making-suffer (creditor-debtor) and inward as the energy of bad conscience (self-torment). The diagram shows it at Level 0, but it reappears at every subsequent level in a new guise. See [[connections/cruelty-sublimated-not-eliminated]] for the full arc from festival to self-torture.

**Will to power as substrate.** Will to power underlies the whole hierarchy — it's the interpretive principle (§12), the external form-giving energy of the state-founders (§17), the internal form-giving of bad conscience turned creative (§18), and the self-torture at maximum guilt (§22). It doesn't sit at one level; it's the force running through all of them.
